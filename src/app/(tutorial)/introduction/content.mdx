# Introduction

<Lead>
An interactive tutorial about how modern AI actually works. The aim is a real, intuitive understanding, not a vague handwave or a wall of equations. No math or computer science background assumed.
</Lead>

The development of AI is possibly the most important thing that has happened in human history. When something is important, it seems important that people understand it.

The goal of this tutorial is to be easily comprehensible to anyone with a middle school education, but give you a really deep intuitive sense of how AI works. Not just **how** it works, but **why** it works this way rather than some other way, and how the ideas behind AI relate to things around you in the rest of the world. I want you to really **get** AI.

The main trick I'm using in achieving that goal is interactive playground. Every new idea has an interactive playground to let you play around with that idea. This adds up to a lot of interactive playgrounds, and it wouldn't have been practical to build so many if I hadn't had AI to help me. Indeed, to some extent, this tutorial is also an experiment to see how AI can help create interactive explainers.

Each chapter also has an optional Google Colab notebook (runs for free in your browser) that gives the real code behind everything explained in the chapters. The notebooks are optional, but I recommend that you try them out if you can.

When I say I want this tutorial to be comprehensible to a middle schooler, I have a particular middle schooler in mind. I've been having my 11 year old son read each chapter and I only consider a chapter "finished" when he fully understands it. Some of the chapters are labelled as "polishing" which means that I don't think he fully understands them yet.

This tutorial isn't yet finished. My eventual goal is for it to cover all the key ideas needed to understand cutting edge AI. Since the cutting edge is moving fast, it's likely this tutorial will never be fully complete.
